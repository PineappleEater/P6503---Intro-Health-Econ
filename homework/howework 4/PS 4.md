Question 1 – Design a Simple CEA (Conceptual + Light Quant) – 25 points
You are advising a national health technology assessment agency on a new oncology
drug, Drug X, versus standard chemotherapy for metastatic breast cancer.
1. Perspective and time horizon (8 points)
a. State the analytic perspective you will use (e.g., payer, societal) and justify your
choice in 2–3 sentences. (4 points)
b. Choose a time horizon and justify it in 2–3 sentences, explicitly referencing the idea of
capturing full value and long-term effects. (4 points)
2. Comparator and outcome type (7 points)
a. Define the comparator and explain why it should be an active standard of care rather
than placebo. (4 points)
b. State whether your primary outcome will be QALYs, life-years, or DALYs, and justify
your choice in 2–3 sentences. (3 points)
3. QALY calculation (10 points)
Suppose:
• Drug X: 3 years at utility 0.75, then 2 years at utility 0.50.
• Comparator: 4 years at utility 0.60, then death.
a. Compute total QALYs for Drug X. Show your arithmetic. (4 points)
b. Compute total QALYs for the comparator. (3 points)
c. Compute incremental QALYs (Drug X – comparator) and interpret in one sentence. (3
points)
Question 2 – Base-Case ICER and Discounting – 20 points
Continue with Drug X vs comparator.
Assume (undiscounted):
• Drug X total cost over lifetime: $200,000
• Comparator total cost over lifetime: $120,000
Use the QALYs you computed in Question 1.
1. Base-case ICER (10 points)
a. Compute incremental cost (Drug X – comparator). (3 points)
b. Using your incremental QALYs from Q1, compute the ICER of Drug X vs
comparator. Show the formula and numbers. (5 points)
c. If the WTP threshold is $100,000 per QALY, is Drug X cost-effective? Answer yes/no

2. Discounting (10 points)
Assume a 3% annual discount rate for both costs and QALYs.
a. Write the present value formula you would use to discount a benefit of 1 QALY
occurring exactly 30 years from now (symbolic formula only). (3 points)
b. Compute the present value numerically (to 2 decimal places). (4 points)
c. In 2–3 sentences, explain how discounting can disadvantage preventive or long-horizon
interventions, linking to the "prevention paradox" idea. (3 points)
